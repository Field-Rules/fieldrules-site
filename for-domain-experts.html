The core innovation
Not just what. Why.

Most systems capture the rule. FieldRules captures the reasoning. The BECAUSE field is what separates a checklist from judgment — and it's the thing only a domain expert can write.

✕ Without BECAUSE
WHEN
Enterprise client requests mid-contract downgrade during active escalation
THEN
Route to retention team first
BECAUSE — [empty]
Just a rule. No reasoning. An AI following this has no idea why — and can't adapt when the situation is slightly different.
✓ With BECAUSE
WHEN
Enterprise client requests mid-contract downgrade during active escalation
THEN
Route to retention team first
BECAUSE
85% of mid-escalation downgrade requests reverse within 72 hours once the root issue is addressed. Processing immediately locks in a lower rate that's harder to unwind and signals to the client that we're not invested in resolving the problem.
Architectural constraint

The BECAUSE field is never pre-filled. Not by AI. Not by templates. Not by prior entries. It exists only when a domain expert writes it. This constraint is permanent and architectural — because the moment you auto-generate reasoning, you've replaced judgment with pattern-matching.
