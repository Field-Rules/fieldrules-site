The Thesis

Everything an agent can do, another agent can replicate.

Between every product and the AI model powering it, there's a layer that determines what context the AI receives, what constraints it operates under, and what reasoning it applies. Most companies haven't built it deliberately. Engineers approximate it from Slack threads. Models fill it from training data.

Fine-tuning changes how the model thinks. Guardrails block what the model says. FieldRules tells the model what to do — and why — based on human-confirmed operational rules. That's a different layer. No one else is building it.

The only thing that can't be replicated is the reasoning your people carry about why your company does things differently. FieldRules makes that layer real before it walks out the door.
Defensibility

Six components. None of them compress.

01
Confirmation Model
Rules require human confirmation before entering the library. No auto-extraction, no assumed consent. Every rule has a named author.
02
BECAUSE Elicitation
Contrastive prompts that extract real reasoning, not post-hoc rationalization. The methodology is the product — not the UI around it.
03
Rule Lifecycle
Full provenance: who authored it, when it was confirmed, how many times it's been applied, whether it's been challenged or revised.
04
Visible Feedback Loop
Domain experts see when their rules are used. PMs query the library before speccing. Engineers read from it at inference time. The loop is the retention mechanism.
05
Skill Graph Provenance
Every rule traces back to the expert who authored it. The company builds an institutional map of who knows what — and it compounds.
06
Eval Instrumentation
The Reasoning Health Score measures whether captured knowledge is genuine reasoning or form-filling. It detects decay before it shows up in product quality.

The confirmed rule library doesn't compress as models improve. It compounds — because your team's hard-earned wisdom compounds.
